The Royal Enfield Continental GT 650 is a retro-styled café racer powered by a 647.95 cc air-cooled, inline twin-cylinder, 4-stroke, SOHC engine that delivers 47.4 PS of power at 7,250 rpm and 52.3 Nm of torque at 5,150 rpm.

It features fuel injection, a wet multi-plate clutch, and a 6-speed manual transmission with a 1-down-5-up gear shifting pattern. The bike has a kerb weight of 214 kg and a total weight of 400 kg, with a fuel tank capacity of 12.5 liters, including a 1.5-liter reserve. It offers a claimed mileage of 27 kmpl and a top speed of 170 kmph.

The Continental GT 650 is equipped with dual-channel ABS, disc brakes at both front and rear (320 mm front, 240 mm rear), and telescopic forks at the front with twin gas-charged shock absorbers with adjustable preload at the rear. It features a steel tubular double cradle frame and spoke wheels with tube-type tyres (front: 100/90-18, rear: 130/70-18). The bike has a saddle height of 804 mm, a wheelbase of 1,398 mm, and a ground clearance of 174 mm. It includes a split seat, passenger footrests, and a combination of analogue and digital instrumentation, including a digital trip meter and digital fuel gauge.

The bike is available in five variants: Rocker Red, British Racing Green, Apex Grey, Slipstream Blue, and Mr Clean, with ex-showroom prices ranging from ₹3,49,609 to ₹3,78,104 in Delhi. It comes with a 3-year or 40,000 km warranty and roadside assistance. The Continental GT 650 also features a Royal Enfield app with a low battery alert and a digital display with hazard warning lights, high beam indicator, and malfunction indicator. The bike has been updated with a full LED headlight, blacked-out engine casing, and alloy wheels with tubeless tyres on select variants, while spoked wheels remain an option for traditionalists.

Owners praise its torquey and tractable engine for highway cruising, stable cornering, and timeless design with good fit and finish. However, common drawbacks noted include its heavy weight, aggressive riding posture, seat discomfort, and engine heat. The bike is considered a strong performer in its segment, balancing retro aesthetics with modern usability and practical features.
